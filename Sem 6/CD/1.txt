what is linux
Linux is a kernel that was created by Linus Torvalds as an open source project. The term is often used to refer to 
operating systems (distributions/distros) that run using 'the kernel'. Some popular distros are RHEL, Fedora, 
SUSE, CentOS, Debian, Arch, Ubuntu, Manjaro, ElementaryOS and Deepin.
